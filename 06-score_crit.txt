# JOB LISTING SCORING CRITERIA
# Stage 06 — LLM Scoring
#
# This file is the single source of truth for all scoring rules.
# Edit values here directly — changes take effect on the next run automatically.
# Lines starting with # are comments and are ignored by the script.
#
# SCORING LOGIC:
#   - Every listing starts at a Fit Score of 100.
#   - Apply all additions first, then all deductions.
#   - Minimum score is 0. There is no ceiling — scores above 100 are valid.
#   - All listings are scored and added to the database regardless of score.
#   - The LLM must return a score breakdown listing every criterion applied.


# ============================================================
# CRITERION 1 — TARGET ROLES
# ============================================================
# Evaluate the job title of the listing against the tiers below.
# If the title does not match any tier, treat as out of scope: deduct 50 points.

PREFERRED TITLES — no deduction:
  - CFO / Chief Financial Officer
  - Finance Director / Director of Finance
  - VP of Finance / VP Finance / Finance VP
  - Group CFO / Group Finance Director
  - Combinations of the above with Legal, HR, IT, or Operations components
    (e.g. CFOO, Head of Finance and Operations, Director of Finance and Legal)
  Spanish equivalents (treat identically to the English titles above):
  - Director Financiero / Director de Finanzas (= Finance Director)
  - VP de Finanzas / VP Finanzas (= VP Finance)
  - Director General Financiero / Director General de Finanzas
  - Combinations of the above with Legal (Jurídico/Legal), HR (Recursos Humanos),
    IT, or Operations (Operaciones)
    (e.g. Director Financiero y de Operaciones, Director Financiero y Jurídico,
    Director Financiero y de Recursos Humanos)

ACCEPTABLE TITLES — no deduction:
  - Head of Finance
  - Any "Group" variant of an acceptable title not already listed above
  Spanish equivalents (treat identically to the English titles above):
  - Jefe de Finanzas (= Head of Finance)
  - Any "Grupo" (Group) variant of an acceptable Spanish title

DISCOUNTED TITLES — deduct 10 points each:
  - Chief of Staff (standalone, no finance component in the title)
  - Fractional CFO / Fractional Finance roles

OUT OF SCOPE — HARD DISQUALIFIER:
  - Any title that does not match any of the above tiers
  Do NOT add a deduction entry for this. Add to the disqualifiers list:
  "Role disqualifier: [job title] does not match any accepted role tier"

# Note on HARD DISQUALIFIERS:

# note is informative, but the listing will never be shown to the user.

# CRITERION 3 — INDUSTRIES
# ============================================================
# Evaluate the industry or sector of the company behind the listing.
# Apply the first matching rule. If no match, treat as neutral.

PREFERRED INDUSTRIES — add 10 points ONCE in total, even if the company matches multiple preferred industries. Do not add 10 per industry — the maximum bonus from this section is +10.
IMPORTANT: the bonus applies only when the listed industry IS THE PRIMARY BUSINESS of the hiring company. A passing mention of a term like "digital transformation" or "automation" in a job description does NOT qualify. Ask: what does this company actually sell or do as its core business?
  - Deeptech
  - SaaS
  - Robotics
  - Aerospace
  - Automation
  - Digital transformation
  - Power generation technologies
  - Pharma research
  - Biotech
  - Healthtech
  - Fintech
  - IoT (Internet of Things)
  - Venture capital
  - Private equity

ACCEPTABLE INDUSTRIES — neutral, no deduction:
  - Industrial manufacturing
  - Any industry not listed elsewhere in this section

DISLIKES — deduct 10 points each (apply all that match):
  - Climate / sustainability focus (as the company's primary mission)
  - E-commerce
  - Quantum computing
  - Cryptocurrency / crypto-related (blockchain, Web3, token-based businesses)


# ============================================================
# CRITERION 4 — COMPANY PROFILE
# ============================================================
# Only apply deductions when clearly determinable from the job description.
# Do not guess or infer beyond what the text states.

CULTURE — deduct 15 points if:
  The role's subject matter is explicitly centred on social impact,
  women-focused initiatives, LGBTQ programmes, DEI roles, or similar.
  Do NOT deduct for generic compliance boilerplate or passing mentions
  of diversity in company values — only deduct when it is the core
  purpose of the role itself.

RECRUITING AGENCY — deduct 10 points if:
  The listing is clearly posted by a recruiting agency or headhunter
  acting on behalf of a client company, rather than the hiring company
  posting directly. Indicators include phrases like "on behalf of our
  client", "our client is looking for", or a known agency/staffing firm
  name in the poster field. If uncertain, do not deduct.


# ============================================================
# CRITERION 5 — LANGUAGE REQUIREMENTS
# ============================================================
# Evaluate language requirements explicitly stated in the job listing.
# If no language requirement is stated: NO deduction.
# If a language is listed only as "advantage", "desirable", "a plus",
# "preferred", "asset", or any other optional framing: NO deduction.
# Apply deductions per language — multiple languages can each trigger a deduction.

# --- FULLY EXEMPT LANGUAGES (NO deduction under any circumstances) ---

ENGLISH — NO deduction at any level, ever.

PORTUGUESE — NO deduction at any level, ever.
  This applies regardless of how it is phrased: "fluent Portuguese required",
  "native Portuguese", "professional proficiency in Portuguese", C1/C2 Portuguese,
  or any other wording. Do NOT deduct for Portuguese under any circumstances.

SPANISH — NO deduction at any level, ever.
  This applies regardless of how it is phrased: "fluent Spanish required",
  "native Spanish", "professional proficiency in Spanish", C1/C2 Spanish,
  "advanced Spanish", or any other wording. Do NOT deduct for Spanish under any circumstances.

# --- LANGUAGES WITH LEVEL-DEPENDENT DEDUCTIONS ---

GERMAN:
  - A1, A2, B1 (basic or intermediary): NO deduction
  - B2 (advanced): deduct 30 points
  - C1, C2, fluent, native, mother tongue, full professional proficiency, or equivalent: deduct 50 points

DUTCH:
  - A1, A2, B1 (basic or intermediary): NO deduction
  - B2, C1, C2, fluent, native, or equivalent: excluded at Stage 05 — will not reach this stage

FRENCH:
  - A1, A2, B1 (basic or intermediary): NO deduction
  - B2, C1, C2, fluent, native, or equivalent: excluded at Stage 05 — will not reach this stage

# --- ALL OTHER LANGUAGES ---
# Any language that is NOT English, Portuguese, Spanish, German, Dutch, or French:

  - A1, A2 (basic): deduct 15 points
  - B1 and above: excluded at Stage 05 — will not reach this stage

# ============================================================
# CRITERION 7 — SCORING SUMMARY RULES
# ============================================================
# These rules govern how the LLM must produce and report the score.

STARTING SCORE: 100

ORDER OF OPERATIONS:
  1. Apply all additions (Criterion 3 industry bonus)
  2. Apply all deductions from all criteria
  3. Floor score at 0 — never return a negative number
  4. No ceiling — scores above 100 are valid

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
  The LLM must return a structured JSON object with the following fields.
  DO NOT calculate or include a total score — Python calculates it from your breakdown.
  Your only job is to identify which criteria apply and list them accurately.

    - company_name    : the name of the RECRUITER or agency if the listing is posted
                        by one (e.g. "SRM Recruitment", "Michael Page"). If the company
                        is posting directly, use the company name.
    - client_company  : if posted by a recruiter/agency AND the actual hiring company
                        is named in the listing, put that company name here. Otherwise
                        leave as empty string "".
    - city_code       : 7-character city code (or fewer if city name is short),
                        uppercase, no spaces, first 7 letters of city name.
                        Use REMOTE for remote roles.
                        Examples: AMSTERD, MUNICH, BARCELO, SAOPAU, BRAGPAU, REMOTE
    - date_published  : date the listing was published (YYYYMMDD format).
                        If unavailable, use date found or date added.
    - job_title       : exact job title as stated in the listing
    - source          : platform where the listing was found (e.g. LinkedIn)
    - additions       : list of additions applied, each with criterion, detail, points
    - deductions      : list of deductions applied, each with criterion, detail, points
    - disqualifiers   : list of any hard disqualifier flags (location or other), or empty list
    - notes           : any additional observations relevant to the listing or scoring
